# BlueCollar UX Research & Usability Testing Report

**Date:** May 30, 2026  
**Conducted by:** BlueCollar UX Team  
**Status:** Research Framework & Testing Plan

---

## Executive Summary

This document outlines the structured usability testing framework for BlueCollar, designed to identify pain points and opportunities for improvement in the user experience for both skilled workers and service seekers.

---

## Research Objectives

1. **Identify pain points** in the current user journey for both workers and customers
2. **Validate core workflows**: finding a worker, sending a tip, creating a listing
3. **Assess platform discoverability** and navigation effectiveness
4. **Evaluate trust signals** and security perceptions
5. **Gather feedback** on mobile-first design and accessibility

---

## Participant Recruitment

### Target Participants: 10 total

#### Group A: Skilled Workers (5 participants)
- **Criteria:**
  - Active in trades (plumbing, electrical, carpentry, welding, HVAC)
  - Age: 25-55
  - Tech comfort: Basic to intermediate
  - Geographic diversity: At least 3 different regions
  - Experience: 3+ years in their trade

- **Recruitment channels:**
  - Local trade associations
  - Social media (Facebook groups for tradespeople)
  - Direct outreach to existing BlueCollar beta users
  - Trade school alumni networks

#### Group B: Service Seekers (5 participants)
- **Criteria:**
  - Homeowners or small business owners
  - Age: 30-65
  - Tech comfort: Intermediate to advanced
  - Geographic diversity: At least 3 different regions
  - Recent experience hiring tradespeople (within 6 months)

- **Recruitment channels:**
  - Community forums and Nextdoor
  - Email lists from home improvement retailers
  - Social media targeted ads
  - Referrals from existing users

## Usability Testing Script

### Session Duration: 60 minutes
### Format: Moderated remote sessions (Zoom/Google Meet)

#### Part 1: Introduction & Warm-up (5 minutes)
- Welcome and thank you
- Explain the purpose: "We're testing the platform, not you"
- Confirm screen recording consent
- Brief overview of what we'll do

#### Part 2: Background Questions (5 minutes)
- Tell me about your experience with [their trade/hiring tradespeople]
- How do you currently find [workers/services]?
- What frustrates you most about the current process?

#### Part 3: Task 1 - Finding a Worker (15 minutes)
**For Service Seekers:**
- "You need a plumber for a leaky faucet. Use BlueCollar to find one."
- Observe: Can they navigate to workers? Do they use search/filters?
- Probe: What information helps you decide? What's missing?

**For Workers:**
- "Browse the platform as if you're looking for job opportunities."
- Observe: Can they understand how to get discovered?
- Probe: What would make you want to list yourself here?

#### Part 4: Task 2 - Sending a Tip (15 minutes)
**For Service Seekers:**
- "You found a worker you like. Send them a $5 tip."
- Observe: Can they locate the tip button? Do they understand the process?
- Probe: What concerns do you have about sending money?

**For Workers:**
- "Imagine a customer wants to send you a tip. Walk through receiving it."
- Observe: Do they understand the wallet connection?
- Probe: How confident are you with blockchain/crypto?

#### Part 5: Task 3 - Creating a Listing (15 minutes)
**For Workers:**
- "Create a profile listing your services."
- Observe: Can they complete the form? What fields confuse them?
- Probe: What information would you want to highlight?

**For Service Seekers:**
- "Post a job request for a service you need."
- Observe: Is the form intuitive? Do they understand next steps?
- Probe: What would make you trust responses?

#### Part 6: Closing Questions (5 minutes)
- Overall, what did you think of BlueCollar?
- What's one thing we should improve?
- Would you use this platform? Why or why not?
- Any final thoughts?

## Key Metrics to Track

### Task Completion
- % of participants who complete each task without assistance
- Time to completion for each task
- Number of errors or backtracking

### Satisfaction
- System Usability Scale (SUS) score
- Net Promoter Score (NPS)
- Likelihood to recommend (1-10 scale)

### Pain Points
- Frequency of confusion or frustration
- Specific UI elements that cause issues
- Navigation patterns that fail

## Testing Environment

- **Platform:** Zoom or Google Meet with screen sharing
- **Device:** Participants use their own devices (mobile/desktop)
- **Duration:** 60 minutes per session
- **Incentive:** $25 gift card or equivalent
- **Recording:** Video + audio (with consent)

## Analysis Framework

### Qualitative Analysis
1. **Thematic coding** of participant feedback
2. **Affinity mapping** to group similar issues
3. **Journey mapping** to visualize pain points
4. **Sentiment analysis** of comments

### Quantitative Analysis
1. **Task success rates** by participant group
2. **Average time-on-task** metrics
3. **SUS score** calculation and benchmarking
4. **NPS distribution** analysis

## Expected Pain Points (Hypothesis)

Based on industry research, we anticipate:

1. **Wallet Complexity:** Users unfamiliar with crypto may struggle with Freighter setup
2. **Trust & Verification:** Service seekers want proof of worker credentials
3. **Mobile Navigation:** Bottom tab bar may not be intuitive for all age groups
4. **Payment Confirmation:** Users may not understand blockchain transaction delays
5. **Profile Completeness:** Workers may not know what information to include
6. **Search Discoverability:** Finding workers in specific categories may be difficult

## Prioritized UX Improvements (Post-Testing)

### High Priority (Implement within 1 sprint)
- [ ] Simplify wallet connection flow with step-by-step guide
- [ ] Add verification badges for workers
- [ ] Improve search filters and categorization
- [ ] Add transaction status notifications

### Medium Priority (Implement within 2 sprints)
- [ ] Create onboarding tutorial for new users
- [ ] Add worker reviews and ratings system
- [ ] Implement in-app messaging between users and workers
- [ ] Add FAQ section for crypto/wallet questions

### Low Priority (Backlog)
- [ ] Advanced analytics dashboard for workers
- [ ] Subscription tiers for premium features
- [ ] Integration with payment processors (Stripe, PayPal)
- [ ] Mobile app (iOS/Android)

## Success Criteria

- **Minimum 70%** task completion rate across all tasks
- **SUS score ≥ 70** (above average usability)
- **NPS ≥ 30** (positive sentiment)
- **No critical blockers** preventing core workflows
- **Actionable insights** for at least 3 UX improvements

## Appendix: SUS Scoring

The System Usability Scale (SUS) is a 10-item questionnaire:

1. I think that I would like to use this system frequently.
2. I found the system unnecessarily complex.
3. I thought the system was easy to use.
4. I think that I would need the support of a technical person to be able to use this system.
5. I found the various functions in this system were well integrated.
6. I thought there was too much inconsistency in this system.
7. I would imagine that most people would learn to use this system very quickly.
8. I found the system very cumbersome to use.
9. I felt very confident using the system.
10. I needed to learn a lot of things before I could get going with this system.

**Scoring:** Each item is rated 1-5 (Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree). SUS score ranges from 0-100.
